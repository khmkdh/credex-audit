# User Interviews

Three conversations with potential users conducted during the week of
2026-05-24. Each interview was 10-15 minutes, conducted in person and
over WhatsApp.

---

## Interview 1 — Aditya M. B., Software Developer (Part-time), AppOpen

**Date:** 2026-05-25
**Duration:** ~12 minutes
**How I reached them:** College classmate, spoken in person

**Summary:**
Aditya is a MCA classmate working part-time as a software developer at AppOpen.
He uses ChatGPT, GitHub Copilot (Individual paid plan), and Claude, both for
his job and personal side projects. He is aware of his spending and generally
satisfied with his current plans.

**Key quotes:**
- "I know what I'm paying and I think it's worth it for the work I do"
- "If this can be fully developed as an app it will be very useful for me
  and my team"
- "Having everything in one place where you can see all your AI tools and
  what you're spending would save a lot of time"

**Most surprising thing they said:**
Aditya was already satisfied with his plans and had no obvious overspending,
which was a useful data point. It confirmed that the tool needs to handle the
"already optimal" case gracefully and not manufacture fake savings just to
seem useful. Users who are spending correctly should be told so confidently.

**What it changed about my design:**
His comment about "fully developed as an app" made me think about the future
potential of SpendSmart as a mobile app where teams track AI spend over time.
For the current version, it reinforced the importance of the "You're spending
well ✓" result being a positive, confident message rather than a dead end.

## Interview 2 — Abhilash B., MCA Student, Course Project

**Date:** 2026-05-25
**Duration:** ~13 minutes
**How I reached them:** College classmate, spoken in person

**Summary:**
Abhilash is a MCA classmate working on a mandatory course curriculum project.
He uses ChatGPT and Cursor on free plans, and pays for Claude's Individual
plan which he uses specifically for his project work. He had not been actively
tracking his AI spending.

**Key quotes:**
- "No wonder I felt like my account balance was getting lower and lower
  without realising much"
- "I didn't think to check how much I was actually spending until you asked"
- "This would have saved me from a few surprises on my bank statement"

**Most surprising thing they said:**
When asked about his monthly AI spend, Abhilash immediately checked his bank
statement mid-conversation and was visibly surprised by the total. He had not
connected his Claude subscription to the gradual drain on his account. This
was the most genuine "aha moment" of all three interviews, he was not aware
he was spending that much until that exact moment.

**What it changed about my design:**
This reinforced the importance of showing the total current monthly spend
prominently on the results page, not just the savings number. Even users who
aren't overspending benefit from seeing what they're actually spending in one
place. Awareness itself is valuable, separate from optimization.

## Interview 3 — Anuska S., Content Creator & Small Business Owner

**Date:** 2026-05-25
**Duration:** ~11 minutes
**How I reached them:** College classmate and close friend, spoken in person

**Summary:**
Anuska is a MCA classmate who is also a professional dancer, runs a small
crochet business and is an Instagram influencer. She uses ChatGPT for
technical purposes and Google Gemini (paid Individual plan) for image
enhancement, high-quality video generation and content curation. She has
even received a paid brand collaboration from Google Gemini. She finds it
difficult to keep track of how much she spends on AI tools monthly.

**Key quotes:**
- "I use Gemini for almost everything related to my content now, it's
  hard to keep track of what I'm actually spending"
- "Since I earn through collaborations and my business, I need to know
  my expenses properly"
- "This tool would be useful not just for developers but for anyone who
  pays for AI"

**Most surprising thing they said:**
Anuska pointed out that SpendSmart could be valuable for content creators
and small business owners, not just developers. She earns money through
paid collaborations and a crochet business, so tracking AI tool expenses
is directly relevant to her profit margins. This was unexpected because the
tool was originally designed with engineering teams in mind.

**What it changed about my design:**
This conversation made me realize the target user is broader than just
CTOs and engineering managers. Content creators, freelancers, and small
business owners who use AI tools for their work have the same pain —
they pay for AI subscriptions without tracking the total. A future version
of SpendSmart should include tools like Canva AI, Adobe Firefly, and
Runway ML that are popular with creators, not just developer-focused tools.
